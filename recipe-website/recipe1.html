Butter Chicken

Butter chicken, or murgh makhani, is a globally renowned Indian dish originating from 1950s Delhi, featuring tandoori-cooked chicken simmered in a rich, creamy, and mildly spiced tomato-butter gravy. It is characterized by its velvety texture, aromatic spices (garam masala, fenugreek), and a balance of tangy and sweet flavors.


When I first tried making butter chicken, I thought the trick was just tossing everything into the pan and letting it bubble away. Big mistake—the chicken turned out chewy, and the sauce tasted sharp instead of velvety. The turning point came when I slowed down: I marinated the chicken overnight, let the tomatoes cook until they lost their tang, and stirred in the cream gently instead of dumping it all at once. Suddenly, the dish transformed into that silky, comforting curry I’d been chasing. My biggest takeaway? Butter chicken rewards patience—don’t rush the marinade, don’t rush the simmer, and always taste as you go. It’s less about following a recipe word-for-word and more about listening to the ingredients as they come together.


This butter chicken recipe is, will be and has always been a showstopper at dinner parties, weddings and any and all events. Beloved of the Indians and the foreigns alike, this recipe will do the trick every time!

Preparation Time: 45 minutes

Difficulty: Medium

Servings: 3

Category: Non-Vegetarian

Ingredients

  • 500g chicken
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 onions (chopped)
  • 2 tomatoes (pureed)
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • Spices (garam masala, turmeric, chili powder)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Marinate chicken with yogurt and spices for 30 minutes.
  2. Heat butter in a pan and sauté onions.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook well.
  4. Add tomato puree and cook until thick.
  5. Add marinated chicken and cook thoroughly.
  6. Simmer until gravy is rich and creamy.
  7. Serve hot with naan or rice.
