Paratha

Paratha is a popular Indian flatbread, often stuffed with potatoes, paneer, or vegetables.


Making parathas taught me the importance of dough texture. Soft dough makes soft parathas.


They are best served hot with butter, curd, or pickle.

Preparation Time: 30 minutes

Difficulty: Easy

Servings: 3

Category: Vegetarian

Ingredients

  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • Water (for kneading)
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 boiled potatoes (mashed)
  • 1 green chili (finely chopped)
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • Fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
  • Salt to taste
  • Butter or ghee for cooking

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix wheat flour and salt. Add water gradually and knead into a soft dough.
  2. Cover and let the dough rest for 15 to 20 minutes.
  3. In another bowl, prepare stuffing by mixing mashed potatoes, spices, green chili, ginger, and coriander.
  4. Divide dough into small balls.
  5. Roll one ball slightly, place stuffing in the center, and seal it.
  6. Roll gently into a flat paratha.
  7. Heat a tawa (pan) and cook the paratha on medium heat.
  8. Apply butter or ghee on both sides and cook until golden brown spots appear.
  9. Serve hot with curd, pickle, or butter.
