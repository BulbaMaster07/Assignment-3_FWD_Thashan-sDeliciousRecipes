Gulab Jamun

Gulab Jamun is a classic Indian dessert made of deep-fried milk solids soaked in sugar syrup flavored with cardamom and rose water.


I learned quickly that the dough consistency matters a lot—too hard and the jamuns crack, too soft and they fall apart. Gentle frying on low heat is the secret.


These soft, melt-in-the-mouth sweets are perfect for festivals and celebrations.

Preparation Time: 40 minutes

Difficulty: Medium

Servings: 3

Category: Vegetarian

Ingredients

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 1/4 cup milk (for kneading)
  • A pinch of baking soda
  • Oil or ghee for deep frying
  • 1.5 cups sugar
  • 1.5 cups water
  • 3-4 cardamom pods (crushed)
  • 1 tsp rose water (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix milk powder, flour, baking soda, and ghee.
  2. Gradually add milk and knead into a soft, smooth dough.
  3. Divide the dough into small equal portions and roll into smooth balls (no cracks).
  4. Heat oil or ghee on low flame for frying.
  5. Fry the balls slowly on low heat until golden brown, stirring continuously.
  6. Meanwhile, prepare sugar syrup by boiling sugar and water with cardamom.
  7. Add rose water to the syrup for flavor.
  8. Remove fried balls and immediately soak them in warm sugar syrup.
  9. Let them soak for at least 2 hours before serving.
